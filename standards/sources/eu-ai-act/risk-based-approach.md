# EU AI Act — Risk-based approach (public summary)

The EU Artificial Intelligence Act establishes a **risk-based regulatory framework** for AI systems placed on the EU market or used in the Union.

## Risk categories

- **Unacceptable risk** — Prohibited practices (e.g., certain social scoring, real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces with limited exceptions).
- **High risk** — Annex III and safety-component systems subject to conformity assessment, quality management, technical documentation, logging, transparency, human oversight, accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity requirements.
- **Limited risk** — Transparency obligations (e.g., informing users they interact with AI).
- **Minimal risk** — No mandatory requirements; voluntary codes of conduct encouraged.

Providers and deployers of high-risk AI must maintain a risk-management system throughout the lifecycle, perform data governance, keep technical documentation, and enable effective human oversight.
